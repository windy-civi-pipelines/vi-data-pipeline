Section 1:
COMMITTEE ON HEALTH, HOSPITALS AND HUMAN
SERVICES
Thirty-Sixth Legislature of the Virgin Islands
May 8, 2025
An act amending title 3 Virgin Islands Code, chapter 25, section 590b, the Caregivers Leave
Act
PROPOSED BY: Senator Novelle E. Francis, Jr.
1 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the Virgin Islands:
2 SECTION 1. Title 3 Virgin Islands Code, chapter 25, section 590b is amended as
3 follows:
4 (a) by striking subsection (b) and inserting the following new subsection (b):
5 “(b) All fulltime employees of the Government of the Virgin Islands, including
6 employees of independent boards, commissions, agencies, autonomous and semi-
7 autonomous agencies, and instrumentalities of the Government of the Virgin Islands who
8 are the primary caregiver of a seriously ill spouse, child, parent, or any individual who is
9 the legal dependent of that employee are entitled to four hours administrative leave per
10 month, each calendar year to perform caregiving duties. Full-time employees of
11 beneficiaries under the Industrial Development Program, and employees of employers
12 who receive benefits under Act No. 4740 who are the primary caregiver of a seriously ill
2
1 spouse, child, parent, or any individual who is the legal dependent of that employee are
2 entitled to two hours leave per month, each calendar year, to perform caregiving duties.”
3 (b) by redesignating subsection (c) as subsection (e) and by adding a new subsection
4 (c) and a subsection (d) as follows:
5 “(c) To be eligible for caregiver’s leave, not more than 48 hours after the absence,
6 the caregiver shall submit proof to the caregiver’s employer that the time off was taken
7 in accordance with the criteria set forth in subsection (b). Failure to submit the proof is
8 sufficient cause for the employer to not pay the caregiver for the time not worked.
9 (d) For the purposes of this section:
10 (1) Caregiver also includes an employee who has been entrusted with or
11 has assumed responsibility for the care of an elder person as defined in 34 V.I.C. §
12 452(k) or a dependent adult as defined in 34 V.I.C. § 452(j).
13 (2) Serious illness means a health condition that carries a high risk of
14 mortality and either negatively impacts a person’s daily functioning or quality of
15 life or excessively strains his or her caregivers.”
16 BILL SUMMARY
17 This bill specifically identifies the caregivers’ duties and expands caregivers leave to
18 include the employees of beneficiaries under the Industrial Development Program and
19 beneficiary under Act No. 4740.
20 BR25-0472/April 3, 2025/SLR
