Section 1:
COMMITTEE ON RULES AND JUDICIARY
Thirty-Sixth Legislature of the Virgin Islands
August 26, 2025
A resolution amending Rule 810(e) and Rule 810(f) of the Rules of the 36th Legislature of the
Virgin Islands to expand the timeframe for filing complaints alleging violations of the standards
of conduct and to revise the provision regarding delayed discovery of violations
PROPOSED BY: Senator Kenneth L. Gittens
1 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the Virgin Islands:
2 SECTION 1. Resolution 1915 (Bill No. 36-0002) adopting the Rules of the 36th
3 Legislature of the Virgin Islands is amended as follows:
4 (a) In Chapter 8, Rule 810(e), by striking the last sentence and inserting:
5 “A complaint may be filed for actions that occurred during the current legislative
6 term, or within 60 days prior to the commencement of the current legislative term, if the
7 accused is a current senator, officer, or employee.”
8 (b) In Chapter 8, Rule 810(f), by striking the first sentence and inserting:
9 “If the Committee determines that the Petitioner did not know, and could not have
10 known of the alleged violation during the timeframe described in subsection (e) through
11 reasonable diligence, the Committee may accept the complaint upon a finding of good
12 cause, but in no case may the complaint be filed more than 12 months after the date of
13 the alleged violation.”
2
1 BILL SUMMARY
2 This resolution amends Resolution 1915 (Bill No. 36-0002) adopting the Rules of the
3 36th Legislature of the Virgin Islands, by amending Rule 810(e) to allow complaints alleging
4 violations of the standards of conduct to be filed for actions that occurred during the current
5 legislative term, or within 60 days prior to its commencement, if the accused is a current
6 senator, officer, or employee, and by amending Rule 810(f) to revise the provision regarding
7 delayed discovery of violations.
8 BR25-0763/August 13, 2025/KEH
