Section 1:
COMMITTEE ON BUDGET, APPROPRIATIONS AND FINANCE
Thirty-Sixth Legislature of the Virgin Islands
February 27, 2025
An Act amending title 24 Virgin Islands Code, chapter 1, section 4, increasing the Virgin
Islands minimum wage
PROPOSED BY: Senators Franklin D. Johnson and Marvin A. Blyden
1 WHEREAS, the Government of the United States first established a minimum wage in
2 1938 with the enactment of the Fair Labor Standards Act of 1938; and
3 WHEREAS, the minimum wage has proven to be one of the most effective and enduring
4 public policy tools to ensure that working people enjoy an acceptable standard of living and
5 quality of life; and
6 WHEREAS, studies have demonstrated that increases in the minimum wage improve
7 worker productivity and workplace retention, and positively affects consumer demand; and
8 WHEREAS, although the federal minimum wage has been raised an average of every
9 three years between 1938 and 2009, it remains stagnant at $7.25 per hour since 2009; and
10 WHEREAS, the Virgin Islands and thirty-three other states and territories of the United
11 States have found it necessary to enact minimum wage increases through legislation or
12 automatic mechanisms for periodic increase; and
2
1 WHEREAS, studies have found that the highest rates of child poverty exist in states that
2 have not raised their minimum wages above the federal standard; and
3 WHEREAS, an economic impact study undertaken by the Virgin Islands Bureau of
4 Economic Research found that the minimum wage increases enacted by Act No. 7856 improved
5 conditions for both workers and businesses in the Virgin Islands; and
6 WHEREAS, Act No. 7856 mandated the appointment of members to the Wage Board
7 which is tasked with periodically reviewing and revising the minimum wage; and
8 WHEREAS, the failure of successive governors to appoint members to the Wage Board
9 has resulted in the Virgin Islands minimum wage remaining at $10.50 since 2018; and
10 WHEREAS, housing market studies have found that housing rents rose by an average of
11 nearly 100% between 2017 and 2023; and
12 WHEREAS, the combination of rising prices and wage stagnation have created an
13 affordability crisis wherein many Virgin Islands families simply cannot meet their daily needs;
14 and
15 WHEREAS, a low minimum wage contributes to an emigration of labor and a reduction
16 in workforce capacity in the Virgin Islands; Now, Therefore
17 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the Virgin Islands:
18 SECTION 1. Title 24 Virgin Islands Code, chapter 1, section 4, is amended as follows:
19 (a) In subsection (a):
20 (1) By inserting a comma after “students”;
21 (2) By striking “$8.35” and inserting “$12.00;
22 (3) By striking “$9.50” and inserting “$14.00”;
23 (4) By striking “June 1, 2017” and inserting “October 1, 2025”;
24 (5) By striking “$10.50” and inserting “$16.00”;
3
1 (6) By striking “June 1, 2018” and inserting “October 1, 2026”;
2 (7) By striking “June 1, 2019 and inserting “October 1, 2027”;
3 (8) By striking “2020” and inserting “2028”; and
4 (9) By inserting at the end of the subsection “, whichever is greater”.
5 (b) In subsection (b) by striking “December 31, 2018” and inserting “December 31,
6 2026”.
7 BILL SUMMARY
8 This bill amends title 24 Virgin Islands Code, chapter 1, section 4 by increasing the
9 Virgin Islands minimum wages.
10 BR25-0269/February 24, 2025/LMW
