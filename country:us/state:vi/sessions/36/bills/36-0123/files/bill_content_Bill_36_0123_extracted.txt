Section 1:
COMMITTEE ON HOMELAND SECURITY, JUSTICE AND
PUBLIC SAFETY
10/09/2025-REPORTED OUT TO THE FLOOR
BILL NO. 36-0123
Thirty-Sixth Legislature of the Virgin Islands
July 23, 2025
An act amending title 20 Virgin Islands Code, part II, chapter 43, subchapter I, section 493
making it unlawful for a person who has 0.04 percent or more, by weight, of alcohol in his
blood to operate a commercial motor vehicle, and making it unlawful for a person under the
age of 21 years who has any detectable amount of alcohol in his blood, to operate a motor
vehicle
PROPOSED BY: Senator Ray Fonseca
1 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the Virgin Islands:
2 SECTION 1. Title 20 Virgin Islands Code, part II, chapter 43, subchapter I, section
3 493 is amended as follows:
4 (a) In subsection (a), add the following new paragraph (3):
5 “(3) Notwithstanding paragraph (2), it is unlawful for any person who has 0.04
6 percent or more, by weight, of alcohol in his blood to drive, operate, or be in actual
7 physical control of, a commercial motor vehicle.
8 (b) In subsection (c), add the following new paragraph (4):
9 “(4) Notwithstanding paragraph (1), any person convicted of violating subsection
10 (a)(3), shall be also subject to the following:
11 (A) A mandatory fine of between $3,000 and not more than $5,000 and the
12 possible requirement to perform up to 30 hours of community service, at the
13 discretion of the court, for a first offense.
2
1 (B) A mandatory fine of between $6,000 and not more than $10,000, and
2 mandatory community service of 60 hours for a second offense within a 10-year
3 period.
4 (C) A mandatory fine of between $10,000 and not more than $15,000,
5 mandatory community service of 100 hours, and mandatory imprisonment for a
6 term of between 30 days and not more than two years for the third or subsequent
7 offenses within a 10-year period.
8 (c) Add the following subsection (g):
9 “(g) The term “commercial motor vehicle” as used in subsection (a)(3) means any
10 self-propelled or towed vehicle used on public highways in intrastate commerce to
11 transport passengers or property when:
12 (1) the vehicle has a gross vehicle weight rating or gross vehicle weight of
13 at least 10,001 pounds, whichever is greater; or
14 (2) the vehicle is designed to carry eight or more passengers, including the
15 driver but not including any commercial motor vehicle licensed, registered and
16 regulated by the Virgin Islands Taxicab Commission; or
17 (3) the vehicle is used in the transportation of materials found by the Virgin
18 Islands Police Department or the Bureau of Motor Vehicles to be hazardous for the
19 purpose of the Hazardous Materials Act (49 U.S.C. sec. 5101 et seq. and
20 transported in a quantity requiring placarding under regulations prescribed by the
21 Secretary of the United States Department of Transportation.”
22 BILL SUMMARY
23 This bill amends title 20 Virgin Islands Code, part II, chapter 43, subchapter I, section
24 493 by making it unlawful for a person who has 0.04 percent or more, by weight, of alcohol in
25 his blood to operate a commercial vehicle, and making it unlawful for a person under the age
26 of 21 years who has any detectable amount of alcohol in his blood, to operate a motor vehicle.
