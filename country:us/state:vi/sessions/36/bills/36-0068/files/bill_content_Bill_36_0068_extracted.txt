Section 1:
COMMITTEE ON BUDGET, APPROPRIATIONS
AND FINANCE
Thirty-Sixth Legislature of the Virgin Islands
March 28, 2025
An Act appropriating $5,000,000 from the General Fund of the Treasury of the Government of
the Virgin Islands to the Virgin Islands Police Department for the purchase and installation of
rapid-shutter motion cameras throughout the territory
PROPOSED BY: Senator Avery L. Lewis
1 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the Virgin Islands:
2 SECTION 1. (a) There is appropriated in the fiscal year ending September 30, 2025,
3 the sum of $5,000,000 from the General Fund of the Treasury of the Government of the Virgin
4 Islands to the Virgin Islands Police Department (“VIPD”) to be allocated as follows:
5 (1) $1,300,000 for the purchase and installation of rapid-shutter motion cameras
6 in the St. Croix district;
7 (2) $1,700,000 for the purchase and installation of rapid-shutter motion cameras
8 in the St. Thomas/St. John district; and
9 (3) $2,000,000 for the purchase, installation, and maintenance of data science
10 software and staff to integrate and modernize the Department’s crime data systems.
11 (b) The funds appropriated in subsection (a) remain available until expended.
12 (c) Fifty percent of the revenues generated from using the rapid-shutter motion
13 cameras in the territory shall be used by the VIPD to reimburse the General Fund and fifty
2
1 percent shall be used to fund the operations of the Real Time Crime Center operations,
2 established in title 3 Virgin Islands Code, chapter 15, section 263a, for equipment,
3 maintenance, salaries, and other related expenses. Once the General Fund has been reimbursed,
4 one hundred percent of the proceeds shall fund the Real Time Crime Center operations.
5 SECTION 2. Not later than 90 days after the installation of the cameras, the
6 Commissioner of the VIPD shall submit a report to the Legislature containing the locations
7 where the cameras were installed throughout the territory.
8 BILL SUMMARY
9 This bill appropriates in the fiscal year ending September 30, 2025, the sum of
10 $5,000,000 from the General Fund of the Treasury of the Government of the Virgin Islands to
11 the Virgin Islands Police Department (“VIPD”) for the purchase and installation of rapid-
12 shutter motion cameras with $1.5 million being allocated to the St. Croix district, $1.7 million
13 being allocated to the St. Thomas/St. John district, and $2,000,000 for the purchase,
14 installation, and maintenance of data science software and staff to integrate and modernize the
15 Department’s crime data systems. The bill provides that the revenues from the use of the rapid-
16 shutter motion cameras be evenly divided for the reimbursement of the $5,000,000
17 appropriation and funding the operations of the Real Time Crime Center operations until the
18 General Fund is fully reimbursed. Thereafter, 100 percent of the proceeds shall go to funding
19 the operations of the Real Time Crime Center operations.
20 The bill also provides for the funds to remain available until expended and for the
21 Commissioner of the VIPD, not later than 90 days after the installation of the rapid-shutter
22 motion cameras, to submit a report to the Legislature containing the locations where the
23 cameras were installed throughout the territory.
